Using AI Ethically in School

Learn how to use AI tools the right way in your academic journey.

Why Does Ethical AI Use Matter?

AI tools like ChatGPT, Grammarly, or image generators can help with schoolwork, but using them without thinking can lead to plagiarism, misinformation, or overdependence. Ethical use means using these tools to help you learn — not to cheat or replace your thinking.

Misuse of AI can also lead to academic consequences. It's important to understand the impact and responsibility of using AI technology wisely.

Examples of Ethical and Unethical Use

Ethical Use Unethical Use
Using AI to brainstorm ideas Submitting AI-generated content as your own
Using AI to understand complex topics Using AI to answer test questions
Grammar and spell-check assistance Faking sources or citations

AI Tools and How to Use Them Right

  • ChatGPT: Ask it to explain ideas, not do your work. Always verify its answers.
  • Grammarly: Great for improving writing, but don’t rely on it for entire rewrites.
  • DALL·E: Generate visuals responsibly. Don’t pass AI art off as original unless it’s modified.
  • Perplexity AI / Bing Chat: Good for quick summaries, but always double-check facts.

Tip: Always cite when you use AI-generated content in schoolwork.
